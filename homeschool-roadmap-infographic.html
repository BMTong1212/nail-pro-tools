The Homeschool
Exploration Roadmap

A 5-stage journey from first curiosity to confident launch — or a well-informed decision to take a different path. Move at your own pace. Stop at any gate.

How to use this roadmap: Each stage ends with a Decision Gate — a brief honest pause before continuing. You do not need to complete all 5 stages to make a good decision. Many families find their answer at Stage 2 or 3. There is no wrong exit.

1Seed
The Seed — Honest Self-Reflection
Weeks 1–2

"Why are we even thinking about this?"

Core Questions to Ask
  • What is driving this — a push from school, or a pull toward something better?
  • Is one parent available full-time, part-time, or do we need a co-op?
  • What does our child say they want?
  • Are our goals academic, faith-based, therapeutic, or all three?
  • What would "success" look like in 5 years?
Top Reasons Families Homeschool
  • Concern about school environment or safety
  • Religious or moral instruction
  • Child with special needs or giftedness
  • Dissatisfaction with academic quality
  • Desire for multilingual / global education
  • Family flexibility (travel, health, faith calendar)
🌱

Gate 1: Can you name 2–3 specific, positive reasons to homeschool — not just frustrations with school? If yes, move to Stage 2. If the answer is only "school failed us," consider advocacy or school change first.

2Explore
The Explorer — Try Before You Commit
Weeks 2–6

No textbooks yet. Low-stakes activities to observe how your child learns naturally.

Ages 3–6
  • Nature walks with a journal or sketchbook
  • Sensory bins (sand, water, rice) with measuring tools
  • Read-aloud with narration back to you
  • Songs and fingerplays in two languages
  • Play-based counting, sorting, patterns
Ages 7–11
  • Unit study on a passion topic (dinosaurs, cooking)
  • Khan Academy 20 min/day — watch frustration tolerance
  • Living book read-alouds (Charlotte Mason style)
  • Simple library science experiment
  • Narration journal: write or draw each day
Ages 12–17
  • 2-week self-directed project on topic of their choice
  • Documentary + family discussion on history/current events
  • Online course trial (Outschool, Duolingo, Coursera)
  • Teach a concept back to a parent or sibling
  • Informational interview with an admired professional
🔍

Gate 2: After 4 weeks of exploration activities, did your child engage more deeply than in a typical school day? Did you enjoy facilitating it? Both should feel like "mostly yes" before continuing.

3Assess
The Assessment — Know Your Child & Yourself
Weeks 4–6

This is where the WorldSeed Curriculum Fit Assessment comes in. Fill it out — one form per child.

What the Assessment Measures
  • Temperament: energy, persistence, adaptability, sensitivity
  • Learning modality: visual, auditory, kinesthetic, reading
  • Gardner intelligence profile (8 types)
  • Language background and multilingual goals
  • Faith formation depth and tradition
  • Parent teaching style and daily capacity
  • Special needs: gifted, dyslexia, ADHD, 2e
Curriculum Philosophies Matched
  • Classical/Trivium — grammar, logic, rhetoric stages
  • Charlotte Mason — living books, narration, nature
  • Montessori — self-directed, hands-on, child-led
  • Waldorf — arts-integrated, developmental rhythm
  • IB / Project-Based — inquiry, global citizenship
  • Eclectic — best-of-all blend (most common!)
📋

Gate 3: Do you have a clear top 1–2 curriculum methods that match your child's profile AND your teaching style? If results feel overwhelming, a homeschool consultant call (often free for one session) is the right next step.

4Trial
The Trial — 4 to 12-Week Test Drive
Weeks 6–18

Run the identified approach with real materials and honest observation — before a full-year commitment.

Sample Weekly Skeleton
  • Mon–Thu: Core subjects: math, language arts, language 2
  • Tue/Thu morning: Science or history unit study
  • Wed afternoon: Co-op, sport, or art outside the home
  • Friday: "Discovery Day" — child-led project or field trip
  • Daily: 20-min read-aloud + 10-min devotional/character
What to Observe & Record
  • Which subjects does your child ask to keep doing?
  • When do shutdowns or refusal happen — and why?
  • Is the parent energized or depleted after each week?
  • Is progress visible even without formal grades?
  • Does the faith/language component feel natural or forced?
🧪

Gate 4: Score 3 things 1–5: child's engagement, parent's sustainability, household harmony. Average ≥ 3.5 → ready to launch. Below 3 → pivot the method before committing to a full year.

5Launch
The Launch — Year One with Confidence
Month 4 onward

Legal foundations, community, and sustainability for a thriving first year.

Legal First Steps
  • File a Notice of Intent with your state/district (HSLDA.org has state guides)
  • Know your state's assessment or portfolio requirements
  • Keep attendance records from day one
  • Join an umbrella school if needed for accredited transcripts
Build Your Village
  • Join 1–2 local co-ops (Classical, faith-based, or secular)
  • Enroll in sport/art/music through community programs
  • Connect with a multilingual homeschool family
  • Schedule regular playdates — socialization is intentional here
Sustain the Educator
  • Plan 1 "personal learning day" per month for yourself
  • Set a non-negotiable end time for school each day
  • Build a school-free week into each term
  • Connect with online communities (Homeschool Village, HSLDA forums)
🎓

You have launched! Revisit the WorldSeed Assessment every 12–18 months. Children's learning styles shift at developmental transitions (ages 7, 10, 13). Your curriculum should grow with them — not stay frozen at year one.

The Middle Path — You Don't Have to Go All-In

For families wanting the best of both worlds

Hybrid Homeschool
School 2–3 days/week, homeschool the rest. Common in Classical model schools.
Micro-School / Pod
3–8 children, one trained educator, parent-funded. Grew 300%+ post-2020.
University Model School
Attend Mon/Wed/Fri; homeschool Tue/Thu with school-issued curriculum.
Online Umbrella
Full accredited program at home via live video (Bridgeway, Connections Academy).
Enrichment at Home
Stay in school; add language, faith, or STEM enrichment in evenings and weekends.
